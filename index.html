# Player Age RAT 365 Watermark Salary Real Value Category Surplus Trade Value End

Methodology

Real values are distributed from a fixed salary pool equal to the sum of every current NBA salary. No money is created or removed — the system only redistributes existing salary based on performance.

The Watermark rating uses a time-decay weighted average: each game's per-game RAT contributes weighted by its season's recency — 2025-26 at full weight (1.0), then decaying by 0.6× per year back (0.6, 0.36, 0.216, 0.13). Recent performance matters most while still crediting a strong track record. Hover over any Watermark to see the per-season breakdown.

The final Real Value is an 92/8 blend: 92% from the Watermark-based distribution and 8% from the RAT 365-based distribution. Both distributions use a soft-capped exponent r^3.2 / (1 + r^3.2/14000), creating a steep progressive curve that rewards elite production while compressing extreme outliers at the top.

A games-played correction is applied per-season to compensate for the Global Rating's built-in availability penalty. For each season, the per-game average is adjusted by (expected / actual)^0.8, capped at 2.5× (floor 1.0×) and only for players with ≥25% of expected games. However, this correction fades with age: players over 28 receive progressively less GP correction, reflecting that older players are more likely to suffer recurring or decline-related injuries. The boost portion decays by e^(-0.12 × (age − 28)), so a 35-year-old retains only ~43% of the correction a younger player would get.

An age factor rewards youth and mildly penalizes age. Players below the league average age (~26.5) receive up to a 2× bonus scaling linearly to the youngest player. Players 35 and older receive a 3% penalty per year over 35 (floor 0.5×). Players between average and 35 are unaffected. After all adjustments, values are renormalized so the total still equals the salary pool.

A potential bonus (up to $18M) is added for players under 25, based on their draft position and age. Both factors use exponential decay: earlier draft picks and younger ages receive larger bonuses, with bigger gaps at the top (e.g., the difference between pick 1 and 2 is larger than between picks 59 and 60). Hover over the Age column to see the potential bonus breakdown.

The distribution uses a soft-capped exponent — score = r^3.2 / (1 + r^3.2 / 14000) — instead of a plain power curve. For mid-tier players, this behaves like a normal r^3.2 distribution, creating enough separation to push ~32 players above max-contract thresholds. For elite outliers, the denominator grows, compressing the very top so no player exceeds ~$95M. The total always equals the salary pool by construction.

Trade Value measures a player's contract-adjusted worth, starting from the 2025-26 surplus. It takes the Real Value as a percentage of the current salary cap, then projects it forward for each remaining contract year using projected future caps and a 5% per year wear-and-tear discount on signed contract years — reflecting the risk of being locked into a guaranteed salary. Player Options: if projected value exceeds option salary, the player declines (PO↗). Team Options: if option salary exceeds projected value, the team declines (TO✗). Restricted Free Agents (YOS ≤ 4 at contract end with positive surplus) get 3 projected years at market salary with 8% raises, at full value with no wear-and-tear (RFA). The league-wide sum of all Trade Values is normalized to $0.

Step 1  Watermark = Σ(weight × games × corrected RAT/g) ÷ Σ(weight × games) — time-decay per season
Step 2  Per-season GP correction: adj/g = raw/g × (1 + (min(2.5, (exp/act)^0.8) − 1) × ageFade)  |  ageFade = e-0.12(age-28) if age > 28, else 1.0
Step 3  score(r) = r^3.2 ÷ (1 + r^3.2 / 14000)  |  TVWM = (score(WM) ÷ Σ) × Pool  |  TVRAT = (score(RAT365) ÷ Σ) × Pool
Step 4  TVblend = 92% × TVWM + 8% × TVRAT
Step 5  Age factor (youth +100%, 35+ −3%/yr) → normalize to Pool
Step 6  Potential bonus = $18M × e-0.05(pick-1) × e-0.5(age-18.82) (under 25 only)
Step 7  Trade Value = Surplus25-26 + Σcontract years ((RV ÷ Capcur) × Capproj × 0.95years out − Salary) + ΣRFA years (full projected − salary)
